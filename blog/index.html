ABOUT

This experiment-oriented blog attempts to bring forth music visualization in both individual and combinational forms of lines, shapes, and colors. "Everyday" from Ariana Grande's latest album "Dangerous Woman" is set as the experimental subject, underpinning eight possible visual performance. Animated representations along with short text explanations are expected to convey the musical messages to a potential audience in a compelling and clear manner, which also seek to serve as promising digital prototypes for concert lighting/stage design. JavaScript and CSS would be employed for this project, an exploration that may lead to intriguing interaction effects. Inspiration is mainly credited to visual artists Zachary Lieberman and Justin Windle.

BIO

Yuqi Wang, born in 1994 in Zhejiang, China. He studied Economics/Management Science at Sun Yat-sen University. Currently majors in Communication Design at Parsons School of Design, focusing on Interaction Design. With enthusiasm for music performance and German culture, he is pursuing future study experience in Lighting/Stage Design in Germany. Lives in New York City, United States.
